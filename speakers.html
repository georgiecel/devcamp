OK, now for the summary of the deets:

  • 🎤 One handheld microphone
  • 🧍 There is a podium/lectern*
  • 🔉 We endeavour to set up lapel mics on the day as well
  • 📺 Aspect ratio of the presentation screen is 16:9
  • 🔌 Connection is HDMI; we will try to provide USB-C-to-HDMI dongles
  • 💻 You can present from your own laptop
  • 🌞 On Wednesday, you have time to test your setup from arrival (~12:30 PM), and on Thursday, from 7:30 AM. Obviously don’t test too close to when the panel or other presentations are happening

*If you think you might need someone to hold a microphone for you for a few slides, or for potential height restrictions on the lectern, let us know ahead of time.

On the day

  • 🔋 Make sure your laptop is charged adquately before your scheduled presentation time
  • ⌚️ Come to the conference room Wednesday from arrival or Thursday from 7:30 AM to test your setup (strongly recommend doing this) or during any of the breaks
  • 🟢 Be ready to plug in when the person scheduled before you is doing their presentation
  • 🎤 While the audience is settling, let the MC know of anything you want them to say in your intro. Your MCs on Wednesday are:
    • Welcome & morning slot (10:00 to 11:00): Rashmita Pinto
    • Late morning slot (11:30 to 12:30): Henry Soesanto
    • Food coma slot (13:45 to 15:00): Shenny Mackie
    • Afternoon slot (15:20 to ~17:20): Chris Wright
  • 🎥 Recording your talk is opt-in, using your own device. It will just be you and the presentation screen in frame. Make sure your device battery is fully charged and has enough storage space. Before your presentation, give it to the person conducting the filming.
  • ❓ You can accept questions from the audience at the end of your session, if there is enough time. Before you start, let the MC know your preference, especially if you would rather not answer questions immediately after your talk.

Helpful tips

  • 🛜 Internet connection can be poor, so make sure you’ve got a local copy of anything you need for demos.
  • 🔊 Consider a video recording for demos. We’ll aim to make sure the sound works, but be prepared to speak to the video in real time if it doesn’t work out.
  • 💬 Please repeat any questions from the audience into the microphone or adequately describe your slides verbally for people who may not be able to see clearly.
  • 💗 Feeling nervous? Some star jumps, going for a walk, or some movement before your talk can help. One thing you can try is to shake each of your limbs 8 times, then repeat but shake them 4 times, then 2, then 1. Making some funny faces can also help relax the jaw.

Backup plan

Only in the event of a crisis! Don’t panic—if something goes wrong, we’ll take our time and work through it, even if it means running a little late or last minute adjustments 😊

  • Save a PDF version of your slides in case your presentation software fails to work
  • Upload this somewhere online as well: email it to yourself, upload it to Slack, etc. so that it may be accessed by someone else somewhere (or use this Google Drive)
  • We can try to load the slides from an organiser’s computer. It is likely to be a MacBook
  • You will do the presentation from someone else’s laptop, but we will allow a couple of minutes beforehand to familiarise yourself with the setup
